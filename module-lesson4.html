Facilitator Notes

Objective: To help students understand that Jesus is fully God and fully man, the Son of God, Savior of the world, and the center of the Christian faith.

Key Scriptures: John 1:1-3, 14; Colossians 1:15-20; Philippians 2:5-11

Topic 3: The Doctrine of Baptism

Matthew 28:19-20 (NKJV) - "Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit..."

What Is Baptism?

Baptism is a public declaration of our faith in Jesus Christ. It's a sign of the new life we have in Jesus and our union with Him in His death, burial, and resurrection.

Baptism is not what saves us, but it demonstrates that we are saved and part of God's family.

Aspect Explanation
Symbol Represents dying to sin and rising to new life in Jesus
Not salvation Baptism doesn't save usâ€”only faith in Jesus does
Obedience It is a step of obedience to Jesus' command
Public declaration Shows others that we now belong to Christ
Spiritual identification We are saying: "I am one with Jesus and His people"
Why Are We Baptized?

Why Are We Baptized?

  • Because Jesus commanded it (Matthew 28:19-20)
  • Because Jesus modeled it (Matthew 3:13-17)
  • Because it demonstrates obedience and faith (Acts 2:41)

What Is Identity in Christ?

Identity is the set of qualities, beliefs, personality traits, or expressions that characterize a person. Identity in Christ is who we are in Christ.

If you are a follower of Jesus Christ, your core identity is that you are a CHILD OF GOD.

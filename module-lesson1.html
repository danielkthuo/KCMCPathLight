Topic 1: The Five Assurances

Objective: To help new believers anchor their faith in unchanging biblical truths that offer spiritual confidence and security.

John 5:24 - "Very truly I tell you, whoever hears my word and believes him who sent me has eternal life and will not be judged but has crossed over from death to life."

The Five Assurances:

1. Assurance of Salvation

1 John 5:11-13 - "God has given us eternal life... whoever has the Son has life."

Key Point: Salvation is a gift received by grace through faith, not by works (Eph 2:8-9).

Application: Help learners understand they are saved the moment they believe in Christ—this is the foundation of their spiritual journey.

Make it clear that:

  • Salvation is not about a list of steps we must follow to earn salvation. Yes, Christians should be baptized. Yes, Christians should publicly confess Christ as Savior. Yes, Christians should practice good works and obey God. However, these are not steps to salvation — they are results of salvation.
  • Salvation is not about following steps. It is about receiving Christ as Savior and recognizing that He has done all the work for us.
  • God requires one step of us — receiving Jesus Christ as our Savior from sin and fully trusting in Him alone as the way of salvation.
  • Why We Need Salvation: Romans 3:23 — Sin separates us from God, who is holy and just. Because of sin, we are spiritually dead and unable to earn or deserve a right standing with Him on our own.
  • How to Receive Salvation: ABC (Admit, Believe, Commit)

Reflection Questions:

Do I truly believe that salvation is a gift, not something I earn?

When doubts arise, where do I turn for assurance?

2. Assurance of Answered Prayer

The assurance of answered prayer comes from knowing that God hears and answers prayers when they align with His will and are prayed in faith.

John 16:24 - "Ask and you will receive, and your joy will be complete."

Key Point: God hears and answers prayers made in Jesus' name, aligned with His will.

  • Key Point: God hears and answers prayers made in Jesus' name, aligned with His will.
  • Jesus was instructing His disciples in these words just before He was crucified. He was preparing them for a new level of relationship with God, where they would pray directly to the Father in His name.
  • "Ask and you will receive" — He was inviting us to come boldly to God in prayer.
  • "In My name" — Not just reciting a phrase as a formula, but praying in accordance with who Jesus is and according to His purposes, rather than our own desires.
  • "Your joy will be complete" — Because answered prayer brings deep spiritual joy as a reminder that God is active and cares for us.
  • Encouragement: "You don't need to have perfect prayers to receive powerful answers. Simply come to God with a willing heart, trusting He sees you, loves you, and replies."
  • Application: Encourage confidence in your prayer life — pray boldly and consistently.
  • 1 John 5:14–15 (NIV): "This is the confidence we have in approaching God: that if we ask anything according to His will, He hears us. And if we know that He hears us—whatever we ask—we know that we have what we asked of Him."

How Prayer Is Answered

  • Immediately at times — Isaiah 65:24
  • Delayed at times — Luke 18:7
  • Different from our desires — 2 Corinthians 12:8–9
  • Beyond our expectations — Ephesians 3:20

Reflection Questions:

Do I approach prayer expecting God to answer?

How can I align my prayers more with God's heart?

3. Assurance of Victory Over Sin

1 Corinthians 10:13 - "God is faithful... He will provide a way out so you can endure it."

Key Point: No temptation is too strong; God provides strength and escape.

Application: Emphasize patience with self—spiritual growth is a process. Stay rooted in God's Word and connected to community.

Reflection Questions:

What sin or temptation do you find most challenging?

What "way of escape" has God shown you in the past?

4. Assurance of Forgiveness

1 John 1:9 – "If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive..."

Assurance of forgiveness is being confident and at peace in your heart that God has forgiven all of your sins because you have placed your faith in Jesus Christ. You can live without guilt or fear anymore — you're free, cleansed, and loved by God.

  • Key Point: Confession brings cleansing; guilt doesn't have to linger.
  • Application: Teach that God's forgiveness is complete freedom that comes through honesty and grace, not shame.
✅ Conclusion:

Having the promise of forgiveness is a huge part of what it means to be a Christian. It means you can have confidence that God has forgiven you because you believe in Jesus — that confidence comes from:


  • God's Word (what the Bible promises)
  • Jesus' completed work on the cross
  • The Holy Spirit residing in you

With this assurance, you have no need for fear or doubting — you can live in peace, joy, and confidence knowing you are truly forgiven and loved by God.

Reflection Question:

Am I holding onto guilt God has already forgiven?

5. Assurance of Guidance

Proverbs 3:5–6 – "Trust in the Lord... and He will make your paths straight."

Key Point: God desires to lead His children daily — guidance flows from trust and surrender.

Application: Build a habit of seeking God in decisions, big or small. Lean not on your own understanding.

The Three Commands:

🔹 1. Trust in the Lord with all your heart

👉 Meaning: Trust God completely. Don't just trust Him part of the time — trust Him with everything in your life: your school, friends, family, and your future.

📌 Example: When things feel confusing, say, "God, I trust You to help me through this."

🧠 Teen Life Example: If you're unsure which subject to pick for next year, or whether a friendship is worth keeping, trust that God sees all sides and will lead you if you trust Him.

🔹 2. Don't lean on your own understanding

👉 Meaning: Don't think you know it all. Your thoughts and feelings can sometimes be wrong. God sees everything, even if you don't. Do not rely only on your own ideas.

📌 Example: Before making a big decision, don't just go with your gut — ask God what He thinks.

🧠 Teen Life Example: You might think, "Everyone is cheating on tests, so it must be okay," but God's way is different. Instead of following what seems right to others, seek what God says is right.

🔹 3. In all your ways acknowledge Him

👉 Meaning: Include God in everything — your school work, talents, friends, activities, hobbies, and choices. Don't just talk to God at church; take Him with you everywhere.

📌 Example: Before you send that text, say, "God, is this the right thing to do?"

🧠 Teen Life Example: Before posting on social media, ask yourself, "Would this PLEASE God?" That's acknowledging Him in all your ways.

✨ The Promise:

"...and He will direct your paths."

If you trust Him, don't rely on just yourself, and acknowledge Him, He promises to guide your life — step by step.

Reflection Questions:

Do I trust God with my future, or lean on my own plans?

How do I make space to hear His voice in daily life?
